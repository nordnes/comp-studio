How options net-of-strike work
You hold 858 options at a strike of $1,572.95 each. Value = (price per share at exit − strike) × options held — never the headline share price alone. Below a $186.7M exit the options are underwater and we show $0, not a hopeful number.
What dilution did to these numbers
Every funding round issues new shares. On the base path (Bridge → Series A → B → C) you keep 48% of today's 1.50% — the figures above already include that. Most offer letters don't; this one does.
Options vs tokens here
Equity is the leveraged leg — worth most in big exits, $0 below the strike threshold. Tokens (0.90% of supply, via Restricted Token Award) carry value in every scenario but unlock late: they vest over 4 years and stay locked until 12 months after TGE, then release monthly over 24 months.
