# Capboard — competitor deep-dive (2026-06-11)

## Snapshot — positioning, target user, company status, pricing

**What it is.** Capboard (https://www.capboard.io/en/) is a Spanish-rooted, EU-first equity-management SaaS: cap table + ESOP/phantom shares + fundraising/exit simulations + investor relations + board meetings + virtual data room + e-signature in one product. Tagline: "Equity management made easy. Give visibility to equity owners of your company, attract and retain top talent." ([home](https://www.capboard.io/en/))

**Positioning.** The budget, all-in-one alternative to Carta/Pulley/Ledgy — they publish head-to-head comparison pages ("Stop Overpaying for Equity Management: Why Capboard Is 3× Cheaper Than Ledgy, Carta, and Pulley", "Capboard vs Carta vs Pulley") and pitch "all the essential equity management features for only €270/year, with open data exports and AI-ready flexibility" ([faq index](https://www.capboard.io/en/faq), [vs-Ledgy page](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-vs-ledgy-startup-equity-management-choice)). Independent confirmation: Waveup's April-2026 tool comparison calls it "the Spanish-rooted, EU-friendly cap-table tool that competes hardest on price… Cheapest serious option in the EU bracket. Built-in VDR is unusual at this price. Solid ESOP/phantom-share modeling." ([Waveup](https://waveup.com/blog/best-cap-table-management-software/))

**Target users.** Four persona tracks surfaced directly in the nav: Founders, LLCs, Investors (portfolio view), Employees (ESOP visibility). Lawyers are a named secondary persona ("For startups and lawyers"). ([home](https://www.capboard.io/en/), [captable](https://www.capboard.io/en/captable/))

**Company status.** Founded by serial SaaS entrepreneurs (CEO Alex Bisbe, ex-Whisbi, acquired by Giosg 2023); "in just two years, we've scaled Capboard globally, with the U.S., Canada, and Spain as our primary markets." Self-reported traction: **$1B+ total assets, 100+ countries, 35K+ stakeholders, 9k+ companies**. EN/ES bilingual; 24/7 "AI Onboarding Assistant" reachable by phone (US/UK + ES numbers). ([about](https://www.capboard.io/en/about/), [home](https://www.capboard.io/en/), [AI assistant](https://www.capboard.io/en/ai-onboarding-assistant-capboard))

**Pricing (verified 2026-06-11; cross-checked against vendor announcement + April-2026 third-party review).**
- **Stakeholder plan:** **$2.00/stakeholder/month billed annually** ($4.00 if billed monthly); **minimum $360/year or $60/month, which includes up to 15 stakeholders**. All features included — pricing scales only by stakeholder count. Effective Dec 1, 2024 (a publicly announced price increase). ([pricing](https://www.capboard.io/en/pricing/) — note: the page renders prices via JS template vars; the concrete numbers come from [Capboard's Dec-2024 pricing notice](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-new-pricing-dec-2024) and are confirmed by [Waveup, verified Apr 2026](https://waveup.com/blog/best-cap-table-management-software/))
- **Scale-up plan:** custom flat annual fee, "ideal for 75+ stakeholder companies or investors"; adds unlimited stakeholders, custom SSO, dedicated CSM, whitelabel, priority onboarding, **self-hosted deployment option**. ([pricing](https://www.capboard.io/en/pricing/))
- **Add-ons:** Premium support $50/mo; Basic Onboarding $300 one-time (≤100 stakeholders, 14-day turnaround); Premium Onboarding custom (100+); **409A valuation $1,800 via partner** (US customers). "Stakeholder" = anyone holding shares/equity; external collaborators (lawyers) are free. 30-day cancellation notice. ([pricing](https://www.capboard.io/en/pricing/))
- No standing free tier (free trial only, "no credit card required"). ([Waveup](https://waveup.com/blog/best-cap-table-management-software/), [captable](https://www.capboard.io/en/captable/))

## Complete feature inventory — by module

Tags: [R] = relevant to Advisor Comp Studio (equity/token comp modeling, tiers, governance, advisor docs), [NR] = not relevant.

1. **Cap table management** [R] — transaction-event model (funding rounds, share issuance, splits/reverse splits, transfers), full equity history timeline, unlimited share classes, authorized shares, "Cap Table by Share Class" view incl. convertibles/SAFEs, "Cap Table by Stakeholder" view with editable columns + historical data tracking, auto-generated share certificates, option to hide company valuation from shareholders, track-changes audit of admin edits. ([captable](https://www.capboard.io/en/captable/), [pricing feature list](https://www.capboard.io/en/pricing/), [stakeholder-view release](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-launches-new-cap-table-stakeholder-view), [share-class release](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-releases-cap-table-updates))
2. **Fundraising & exit simulations (scenario modeling)** [R — closest analogue to our Conservative/Base/Aggressive scenario walk] — "Make projections of dilution and voting rights with different funding rounds and exit scenarios. Calculate payouts taking into account your current cap table." Key mechanics: simulations are **private** (not visible to stakeholders), you can **stack several scenarios** ("Have you received several term sheets? Create simulations with all scenarios"), stack multiple rounds, then simulate a subsequent acquisition and compute **per-shareholder and per-employee payouts**. UI path: *Ownership → Simulations → "+ Create Simulation"* (button top-right). ([captable](https://www.capboard.io/en/captable/), [simulation FAQ](https://www.capboard.io/en/faq/how-to-create-a-simulation))
3. **ESOP / employee equity plans** [R] — pool creation ("assign a % of fully diluted equity and set the main terms — vesting, cliff… we take care of the rest"); grant instruments: **stock options, phantom shares, warrants, SARs, RSAs/RSUs**; bulk-automated grant letters from templates (or upload your own) with compliant e-signature; auto vesting calculation incl. multiple grants & vesting types per employee, milestone-based vesting, Post-Termination Exercise Period (PTEP); exercising workflow; "compliant all over the world… updated to the country's legal requirements where your company is registered"; HR-tool integration for on/offboarding. ([equity plans](https://www.capboard.io/en/automate-equity-plans/), [pricing](https://www.capboard.io/en/pricing/), [ESOP mgmt](https://www.capboard.io/en/esop-management-for-startups), [PTEP release](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-september-updates-ptep-alerts))
4. **Stakeholder/employee portal with self-serve exit simulations** [R — directly parallels our advisor-facing Proposition] — equity holders log in and see only their own grants: "full visibility on vesting schedule, cliff, good and bad leaver clauses" ([VC Stack](https://www.vcstack.io/product/capboard)); "employees have full visibility on their equity grants and **can simulate exit scenarios**, increasing their retention" ([captable page / search corpus](https://www.capboard.io/en/captable)); automated emails "every time they receive a new grant, vest equity, or their equity value changes"; education content built into the portal pitch (Build trust / Reward / Educate). Public how-to FAQs: ["How to visualize my ownership"](https://www.capboard.io/en/faq/how-to-visualize-my-ownership), ["How to invite stakeholders"](https://www.capboard.io/en/inviting-stakeholders-to-capboard), ["Stakeholder change requests"](https://www.capboard.io/en/faq/stakeholders-change-requests) (stakeholders propose data corrections, admin approves). ([equity plans](https://www.capboard.io/en/automate-equity-plans/), [ESOP visibility](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-esop-visibility-empowers-employees))
5. **Advisory shares** [R — their direct content play on our exact domain] — a long founder guide covering stock options vs RSAs for advisors, **typical advisor vesting of 1–2 years with no/3-month cliff**, **typical grant size 0.1%–0.25% (up to 1%)**, links to the **FAST agreement** (fi.co/fast), advisory-board construction, dilution caveats, and "automate the process of issuing advisory shares" with Capboard. No dedicated advisor *module* — advisors are handled as a grant type inside ESOP tooling. ([advisory shares](https://www.capboard.io/en/captable/advisory-shares))
6. **SAFEs & convertibles** [R-partial — conversion math across scenarios] — SAFE/convertible-note management, post-money SAFE conversion walkthrough, SPV financing support + SPV-investor visibility. ([SAFE page](https://www.capboard.io/en/captable/safe-convertible), [post-money SAFE FAQ](https://www.capboard.io/en/faq-post-money-safe-conversion), [SPV FAQ](https://www.capboard.io/en/faq/visibility-for-spv-investors))
7. **Valuations / 409A** [R-partial — valuation inputs drive our FDV scenarios] — add unlimited company valuations (visible or hidden to shareholders); duplicate/valuation alerts; **409A delivered by a partner for $1,800** with "formal valuation opinion… IRS-compliant," marketed as audit-ready; comparison page vs Carta's 409A plans. Not an in-house valuation engine. ([409A](https://www.capboard.io/en/409a-valuation-us-startup-equity-common-shares), [pricing](https://www.capboard.io/en/pricing/), [vs Carta 409A](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-vs-carta-pricing-409a-and-features-comparison))
8. **Investor relations / updates** [NR] — templated investor updates with KPIs and pre-defined formatting; engagement analytics (reading time, shares, clicks); stakeholder grouping; market-news/press/product-update feeds. ([investor relations](https://www.capboard.io/en/investor-relations/))
9. **Board & shareholder meetings** [R-partial — our governance/consents checklist analogue] — meeting types (board/shareholder/custom), notice-day rules, attendee management auto-fed from cap-table data, **voting records + delegated representation (proxies)**, minutes capture, e-signature of meeting documents, members-only access + encryption. ([board meetings](https://www.capboard.io/en/board-shareholder-meetings/))
10. **Virtual data room** [NR mostly; watermarking/doc-tracking is adjacent] — folder/file-level granular permissions by user or group, password protection, access notifications + dealflow-prioritizing analytics, document encryption ("deleted from backups when removed"), SSO-gated access, cap table + funding history embeddable into the VDR. ([dataroom](https://www.capboard.io/en/dataroom/))
11. **Contracts & e-signature** [R — our Proposition doc + signature flow] — SAFE/ESOP/other contract template library, bulk contract generation, **Capboard's own e-signature solution**, custom document templates + signing (review-confirmed). ([pricing](https://www.capboard.io/en/pricing/), [e-sig FAQ](https://www.capboard.io/en/faq/contract-signatures), [Capterra review](https://www.capterra.com/p/248720/Capboard/reviews/))
12. **Roles, permissions & audit** [R — our audit-log analogue] — four hardwired roles: Founders (full), Investors/advisers (see companies they hold), Employees (own grants only), Collaborators/lawyers (everything except billing); "**access and change monitoring service, so we keep a history of what users have seen or accessed**"; Track Changes on admin edits. ([pricing FAQ](https://www.capboard.io/en/pricing/))
13. **Country-specific & entity coverage** [NR] — LLC support (membership units), "Libro de Socios" (Spanish shareholders' book) download, LATAM positioning (bilingual, multi-currency), country-adapted legal requirements, 2026 US QSBS-compliance content. ([LLC page](https://www.capboard.io/en/llc-simplified-capboard-equity-management), [Sept update](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-september-updates-ptep-alerts), [LATAM](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-io-best-cap-table-management-platform-for-latam-startups))
14. **Free tools / lead-gen calculators** [R — pattern for a public-facing comp calculator] — Cap Table Calculator (`/en/captable/create`), Equity Dilution Calculator, cap-table templates, startup-valuation guide, investor list, ESOP/phantom/vesting guides, investor-update templates. ([resources nav](https://www.capboard.io/en/faq), [dilution calc](https://www.capboard.io/en/captable/equity-dilution-calculator))
15. **Investor portfolio view** [NR] — real-time multi-company portfolio tracking for investors. ([portfolio view](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-new-investment-portfolio-view))

## UI/UX documentation

**IA / navigation.** The marketing site reveals the app's IA: top-level product areas are **Cap Table (Ownership) · Equity Plans (ESOP) · Investor Relations · Board & Stakeholder Meetings · Data Room · Simulations**. The documented in-app path *"Ownership → Simulations → '+ Create Simulation' (top right corner)"* shows a classic two-level sidebar-section + primary-action-button layout. ([simulation FAQ](https://www.capboard.io/en/faq/how-to-create-a-simulation))

**Key screens (from product pages, release notes, and FAQ screenshots — screenshots are embedded base64 PNGs on FAQ pages, confirming real-UI documentation):**
- *Cap Table by Stakeholder view:* spreadsheet-like table with **editable/configurable columns, historical data tracking, and ESOP integration in the same grid** — i.e., point-in-time cap table browsing. ([release note](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-launches-new-cap-table-stakeholder-view))
- *Cap Table by Share Class view:* per-class rollup including convertibles/SAFEs as their own rows. ([June-2024 release](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-releases-cap-table-updates))
- *Add-transaction flow:* "Add funding events and other transactions" — an event-type picker modal (their May-2024 notes mention an "updated equity grant management modal" and "new data integrity rules"), i.e., forms are modal-driven with validation guards. ([captable](https://www.capboard.io/en/captable/), [May-2024 release](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-may-2024-release-notes))
- *Simulations:* create a named private scenario from the live cap table; stack rounds; toggle to exit payout calculation per holder. ([simulation FAQ](https://www.capboard.io/en/faq/how-to-create-a-simulation))
- *Enhanced ESOP Grants view:* "user-friendly interface with detailed grant management features" — a grants list with drill-in per-grant detail (vesting schedule, cliff, leaver clauses, PTEP). ([grants-view release](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-enhanced-esops-grants-view))
- *Employee/stakeholder portal:* "How to visualize my ownership" — an ownership dashboard per stakeholder showing grants, vesting progress, current value, with exit-scenario simulation; automated notification emails on grant/vest/value-change events. ([ownership FAQ](https://www.capboard.io/en/faq/how-to-visualize-my-ownership), [equity plans](https://www.capboard.io/en/automate-equity-plans/))
- *Board meetings:* a 5-step segmented flow (Meeting type → Attendees & notice → Voting & delegation → Minutes → E-signature/doc-sharing), each step a card with icon — wizard-grammar for a legal workflow. ([board meetings](https://www.capboard.io/en/board-shareholder-meetings/))

**Forms/wizards.** Heavy template-first design: grant letters, investor updates, contracts and meeting notices all start from templates ("Duplicate, replace and send!"). Plan creation is deliberately minimal — "assign a % of fully diluted equity and set the main terms (vesting, cliff…). We will take care of the rest." ([equity plans](https://www.capboard.io/en/automate-equity-plans/), [investor relations](https://www.capboard.io/en/investor-relations/))

**Charts & interactions.** Marketing/visible UI shows pie/donut ownership charts, vesting timelines and tabular cap tables rather than exotic visualizations; the analytical depth is in tables + scenario math, not charting. (Inference from product imagery on [captable](https://www.capboard.io/en/captable/) and FAQ screenshots; no public chart-library evidence.) [uncertain]

**Visual tone.** Clean, friendly SaaS: white background, single accent color, icon-led feature cards, generous whitespace, emoji in product comms ("✈️ Autopilot", "🆕", "✨ AI Onboarding Assistant"). Two site generations coexist (WordPress marketing shell + an older app-styled subsite), suggesting iterative redesign.

## Known criticisms & limitations (review-sourced)

- **4× price increase backlash:** "Price increase 4x at the end of last year. It was a more cost effective option which is why we chose it but now it's right up there with the top competitors so I feel like we could have just gone with a more well known product." — Capterra, Feb 2025, 3★. Corroborated by Capboard's own [Dec-2024 pricing notice](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-new-pricing-dec-2024) applying new rates to *existing* customers at renewal. ([Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/248720/Capboard/reviews/))
- **Early-product gaps:** "It's an early product so there are convenient features that are not there yet." — Capterra 5★. ([Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/248720/Capboard/reviews/))
- **Missing local-compliance automation:** "Some features could be automated. Like the HMRC filings and SEIS filings." (UK). ([Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/248720/Capboard/reviews/))
- **Thin docs/tutorials:** "More detailed reference material on the main features"; "would suggest more tutorial." ([Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/248720/Capboard/reviews/))
- **No in-house valuation engine:** 409A outsourced to a partner ($1,800); one reviewer wanted a built-in pre/post-money valuation report service. ([pricing](https://www.capboard.io/en/pricing/), [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/248720/Capboard/reviews/))
- **Smaller US/law-firm footprint:** "if your law firm is Delaware-default, expect some manual reconciliation"; "Eqvista, Capboard, and Cake Equity sometimes require a workflow conversation up front" with law firms. ([Waveup, Apr 2026](https://waveup.com/blog/best-cap-table-management-software/))
- **Tiny review base** (10 Capterra, ~3 G2) — low independent validation vs Carta/Pulley. ([Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/248720/Capboard/reviews/), [G2](https://www.g2.com/products/capboard/reviews))

## Data, benchmarks & methodology

Capboard publishes no compensation benchmarks. Its closest "methodology" assets are editorial heuristics: advisor grants "typically 0.1%–0.25%, up to 1%"; advisor vesting "1–2 years, no cliff or a short 3-month cliff"; pointer to the **FAST template** as the canonical advisor agreement. ([advisory shares](https://www.capboard.io/en/captable/advisory-shares)) Simulation methodology is deterministic cap-table math (dilution, conversion, liquidation-preference seniority — they publish a seniority/liquidation-preference explainer) rather than probabilistic modeling. ([seniority guide](https://www.capboard.io/en/seniority-in-liquidation-preference)) No compa-ratio, banding, or survey-data features — a genuine gap vs Advisor Comp Studio's tier/guardrail engine.

## Patterns worth borrowing for Advisor Comp Studio

1. **Private, stackable named simulations with a payout view** — "create a simulation… simulations are private… stack several scenarios… calculate payouts after an exit." Maps cleanly onto our Conservative/Base/Aggressive scenario sets: persist each scenario as a named object, allow stacking (e.g., bridge → Series C → exit), and keep drafts invisible to the advisor until shared. ([simulation FAQ](https://www.capboard.io/en/faq/how-to-create-a-simulation), [captable](https://www.capboard.io/en/captable/))
2. **Per-recipient self-serve outcome view** — the employee portal shows *only that person's* grants with vesting progress, live value, and self-run exit simulations. Our printable Proposition could gain an interactive twin: a read-only advisor view of *their* package walked across our three scenarios, always net-of-strike. ([equity plans](https://www.capboard.io/en/automate-equity-plans/), [ownership FAQ](https://www.capboard.io/en/faq/how-to-visualize-my-ownership))
3. **Event-driven notifications on grant/vest/value-change** — "automated emails every time they receive a new grant, vest equity, or their equity value changes." Borrow as in-app changelog entries in our audit log when a draft's underlying valuation or vesting state changes. ([equity plans](https://www.capboard.io/en/automate-equity-plans/))
4. **Five-step segmented governance wizard** — board-meeting flow (type → attendees/notice → voting/delegation → minutes → e-sign) is a clean grammar for our governance/consents checklist: each consent step a card with status, owner, and document attach. ([board meetings](https://www.capboard.io/en/board-shareholder-meetings/))
5. **"Track changes" + viewed-history audit** — they log both admin *edits* and what users have *seen/accessed*. Our audit log currently covers edits; adding a "viewed by" trail on the Proposition doc would strengthen the confidentiality story. ([pricing FAQ](https://www.capboard.io/en/pricing/))
6. **Editable-column, point-in-time table view** — the Stakeholder cap-table view's configurable columns + historical snapshots is a good model for our advisor roster: per-tier columns, toggleable, with an "as-of" date scrubber. ([stakeholder-view release](https://www.capboard.io/en/capboard-launches-new-cap-table-stakeholder-view))
7. **Inline concept education** — a reviewer explicitly asked them to "explain some concepts that appear (vesting, cliffs, etc.)." We should preempt this: tooltip glossary on strike/net-of-strike/FDV/cliff in every advisor-facing surface. ([Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/248720/Capboard/reviews/))
8. **Template-first document generation with bulk e-sign** — grant letters generated from templates in bulk, signed in-platform. Pattern for evolving our watermarked Proposition from print-only to a template + (future) signature flow — while keeping "discussion draft, not a binding offer" framing, unlike their binding grant letters. ([equity plans](https://www.capboard.io/en/automate-equity-plans/), [dataroom](https://www.capboard.io/en/dataroom/))
