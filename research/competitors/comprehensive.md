# Comprehensive — competitor deep-dive (2026-06-11)

> Target: **Comprehensive** (Comprehensive.io) — https://www.comprehensive.io/ · focus page: https://www.comprehensive.io/products/compensation-management
> Relevance tags are scored against **Advisor Comp Studio** (advisory-board equity net-of-strike + tokens, scenario walks, FAST tiers, compa-ratio guardrails, governance checklist, audit log, watermarked Proposition doc). [R] = relevant pattern, [NR] = not relevant.

## Snapshot — positioning, target user, status, pricing

- **What it is:** "The AI-powered, fully-customizable compensation platform" — plan, communicate, and benchmark compensation "with full configurability – and zero spreadsheets. #PayPeople" (homepage hero, fetched 2026-06-11: https://www.comprehensive.io/). Three pillars: compensation review cycles, benchmarking data, and employee total-rewards communication.
- **Origin & founders:** Founded 2022 by **Roger Lee** (CEO; creator of Layoffs.fyi, co-founder of Human Interest) and **Teddy Sherrill** (CTO; ex-CTO Restaurant Brands International). Launched publicly Jan 2023 as a free pay-transparency tracker days after California's pay-range disclosure law took effect (https://www.hrdive.com/news/pay-transparency-tracker-California-NYC/640103/, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roger_Lee_(entrepreneur)).
- **Funding:** $6M seed (2022) led by Inspired Capital with Floodgate, SV Angel; ~$15M total raised per Crunchbase (https://techcrunch.com/2022/10/04/this-startup-wants-to-help-other-startups-figure-out-compensation/, https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/comprehensive). [uncertain — total figure is aggregator-sourced]
- **Target user:** HR/People + Finance teams at **~100–2,000-employee** companies (G2 reviewers are overwhelmingly "Mid-Market 51–1000 emp."; https://www.g2.com/products/comprehensive/reviews). Customers shown: Supercell, Linux Foundation, VGW, 15Five, Clever, Alloy, HopSkipDrive, LandSouth Construction (homepage logo wall).
- **Status (2026-06-11):** Active; G2 **4.9/5 from 28 reviews**, badges for High Performer / Best Support / Easiest To Do Business With (https://www.g2.com/products/comprehensive/reviews; homepage badges). Newest visible features are AI-branded (analytics Q&A, manager coaching, AI job matching).
- **Pricing:** Not published. Per their own FAQ: **per-employee-per-month, billed annually, USD**, headcount auto-tracked via HRIS and prorated; pricing does not vary by user type; one-time implementation fee "can often be waived"; annual contracts with high-touch support (https://www.comprehensive.io/faq, "Pricing & Contracts"). No public dollar figures anywhere. [uncertain — actual $ amounts]
- **Implementation claims:** "No-touch implementation… operational in less than 2 weeks" (homepage); FAQ says most implementations complete in **2–4 weeks**, some go live "in as little as a few days," and companies typically start 1–3 months before a cycle (https://www.comprehensive.io/faq).

## Complete feature inventory — by module

### 1. Compensation review cycles (core module) — https://www.comprehensive.io/products/compensation-management
- [R] **Guided cycle workflow** replacing spreadsheets: one cycle can bundle promotions, base salary changes, bonus targets, bonus payouts, and equity grants in a single configured workflow (page FAQ).
- [R] **Merit/suggestion logic engine:** formula-driven suggested increases using performance ratings, **compa-ratio positioning**, tenure eligibility, and custom formulas; fields can be fully editable, formula-driven, or mixed (page FAQ).
- [R] **Guardrails:** caps, floors, and range-based guardrails with **optional justification requirements** when exceeded; hard caps vs. soft warnings configurable per field (page FAQ; https://www.comprehensive.io/faq "Configuration & User Experience").
- [R] **Budgets:** fixed % pools, dynamic budgets computed from suggested increases, or combined merit+promotion pools; budgets computed per department/country via eligibility rules; managers see total budget + real-time usage; overruns trigger visual warnings + justification + approval routing (FAQ "Budgeting").
- [NR] **Approval chains:** configurable by role/hierarchy/org structure, editable mid-cycle; proposer/approver split (managers propose, HR/leadership approve); batch approvals with per-employee edit; geography-specific workflows via HRIS attributes; nudge notifications (page FAQ).
- [R] **Mid-cycle mutability with employee shielding:** rules/formulas/configs can change during an active cycle; "employees don't see changes until award letters are released" (page FAQ).
- [R] **Scenario planning** is listed under "Easy administration" (checklist on page); a G2 reviewer confirms "the ability to model out potential year-end scenarios with centralized rule-sets" (https://www.g2.com/products/comprehensive/reviews, review #28).
- [NR] Eligibility rules (hire date, employment type, country, performance); contractor exclusion; leave/termination handling synced from HRIS.

### 2. Pay ranges / bands — same URL
- [R] Single source of truth for ranges; import/maintain/update in-platform; map ranges to job titles or title groups; **selective sharing based on permissions** ("no more miscellaneous versions floating around" — homepage).
- [R] **Compa-ratio modeling:** "Comprehensive can model compa-ratio philosophies and display employee positioning within ranges"; benchmark targets adjustable to internal leveling/grading frameworks (page FAQ). Ranges are *optional* — cycles can run without them and add them later.
- [R] Visual level-comparison graphics (range bars across levels — marketing screenshots on page).

### 3. Total Rewards dashboard — same URL
- [R] Employee-facing dashboard showing base, bonus, variable, **equity**, benefits, PTO; local currency with configurable FX conversion; customizable by group/geography; launched typically post-cycle (page FAQ).
- [R] **"Model the value of equity"** is an explicit employee-communication feature (checklist). Via Carta: "Let employees easily model the potential value of their equity under various scenarios, including a clear breakdown of vested vs. unvested shares" (https://www.comprehensive.io/partners/carta).

### 4. Employee communications / award letters — same URL
- [R] Configurable letter templates referencing calculated values (e.g., % increase) with per-field visibility toggles; delivered as **web documents with optional PDF download**; delivery + acknowledgement tracking with bulk reminders; letters can be generated from uploaded data *without* running a cycle ("structured alternative to manual mail merges") (page FAQ).
- [R] **AI manager coaching:** "personalized talking points for every employee conversation" (homepage "AI built in" section).

### 5. Analytics — same URL
- [R] Pay-equity reporting; demographic segmentation (gender/race/age); promotion-rate analysis; market-position distribution views; FTE normalization; **rule-based outlier flagging**; **audit log**; CSV export; "AI-powered data insights — ask any question to surface immediate insights from your comp data" (page checklist + FAQ).
- [R] **EU pay-transparency analytics:** "Comprehensive supports analytics needed for EU pay transparency requirements, with reporting capabilities that continue to expand" (page FAQ).

### 6. Benchmarking — https://www.comprehensive.io/products/benchmarking
Four datasets in one search UI: (a) **Comprehensive job-postings data** — real-time ranges from 6,000+ US tech companies, free; (b) **Mercer Comptryx Global Tech** — 4M+ employees, 100+ countries (https://www.comprehensive.io/partners/mercer); (c) **Salary.com** — 15,000 titles, 225 industry breakouts, 32,000+ compensable factors (https://www.comprehensive.io/partners/salary-dot-com); (d) **Executive Compensation Survey** (below). Features: intuitive search by job function/title, filters (location, industry, org size), **level comparisons** ("unified view of percentile data across all levels"), full pay spectrum (base, STI, LTI), **AI-powered job matching** across data sources, blend multiple benchmarks for niche roles. ## UI/UX documentation

**IA / navigation:** Marketing site is Webflow with a thin top nav (Compensation Management · Benchmarking · Integrations · Login · Free Data · Schedule Demo). The product app lives at app.comprehensive.io. Product IA per screenshots and FAQ: admin cycle builder → manager review table → approver batch view → employee Total Rewards portal; benchmarking is a sibling search surface.

**Key screens (from marketing screenshots + reviews):**
- *Cycle review table:* spreadsheet-like grid per manager — employee rows with performance rating, promotion status, salary change, equity and bonus columns; admins control "which columns appear by default, which are optional, and how information is organized to keep the UI clean"; columns can be "added, removed, or reordered to mirror existing spreadsheet workflows" (https://www.comprehensive.io/faq). Customizable table views **per role**.
- *Budget bar:* managers see total available budget and real-time usage while recommending; overruns flag red. One reviewer criticism is instructive: "When a specific action causes a line item to show as 'over,' even if the overall budget remains within limits, the red indicators can create unnecessary alarm" (G2, Molly S., Mar 2026 — https://www.g2.com/products/comprehensive/reviews).
- *Total Rewards dashboard:* stacked breakout of salary / equity / variable / bonus / benefits per employee, with equity scenario modeling (Carta page screenshots).
- *Pay ranges screen:* ranges across job family × level rendered as horizontal band bars with employee position markers (benchmarking page graphics).
- *Award letter flow:* preview + email letters directly from the system ("instead of a batch download" — G2, Marwa H. & Mikel A. M., Jul 2025); admin tracker of sent/acknowledged letters with bulk reminders.
- *Exec survey UI:* filter rail (capital raised, revenue, headcount, valuation, round) + scatter of individual anonymized data points + percentile summaries (exec-survey page screenshots).

**Pay Range Tracker UI (public app):** The free tool redirects to `app.comprehensive.io/benchmarking/postings` and renders per-job-family pages. The default page title observed on fetch (2026-06-11): **"Software Engineering Salary - IC3 (Proficient) | $140k - $187k"** with meta "The average Software Engineering salary range disclosed in job posts is $140k - $187k for IC3 (as of Jun 2026)" — i.e., the UI is organized as **job family → level (IC1…IC-n with plain-language labels like "Proficient") → aggregated min–max of posted ranges, stamped with an as-of month**. The marketing homepage embeds a live "Choose Job Family" picker that loads results inline. Early coverage describes browsing **by company and by range**, including leaderboard-style tables of highest/lowest posted ranges per role (Tesla $83k–$418k senior SWE example) and CA/NYC compliance percentages (https://blog.pragmaticengineer.com/the-scoop-salary-transparency/, https://www.hrdive.com/news/pay-transparency-tracker-California-NYC/640103/, https://www.ciodive.com/news/pay-transparency-tracker-California-NYC/639955/). SEO sitemap of per-role pages at https://app.comprehensive.io/benchmarking/web-sitemap. The app is a React SPA, so deep UI inventory (exact filter chips, table columns) is not crawlable without a browser. [uncertain — current filter set beyond job family/level/location]

**Charts & interactions:** range-band bars with markers (pay ranges), percentile level ladders (benchmarking), stacked total-rewards breakdowns, scatter of individual exec datapoints, budget gauges with red over-budget states. Reviewer: "the analytics were helpful but could use some changes to improve graphic layout (order or groupings of data)" (G2, Mikel A. M.).

**Forms/wizards:** cycle setup is admin-configured (logic builder: "The logic builder to create the calculation logic is easy to use and enables customising our calculation logic" — G2, Michael C., Aug 2024); justification prompts are injected contextually when a manager exceeds guidelines or proposes off-cycle promotions.

**Visual tone:** clean, light, rounded SaaS aesthetic; pastel accent checks (green/orange/blue/violet) per feature group; product praised as "clean… the pages looked in Comprehensive" and "professional, easy to understand" (G2 #25, #22). Manager UX is the showpiece; admin UX lags (below).

## Known criticisms & limitations (review-sourced)

Coverage is **thin overall** — only 28 G2 reviews, skewing 4.5–5.0, mid-market; no meaningful Capterra/TrustRadius footprint found. Recurring negatives from https://www.g2.com/products/comprehensive/reviews:
- **Admin backend not intuitive / steep learning curve:** "the manager experience is incredibly intuitive, but I wish it was a little more-so for admins" (Megan G., Nov 2024); "Navigating as an admin can be somewhat confusing" (Joelle B.); "requires a bit of administration from the account management team to do some of the things I'd like to do myself" (#28). Setup of some features "required a bit more technical expertise than expected" (Joanne B.).
- **Reporting/analytics flexibility:** "some reporting needs can still require extra configuration, exports, or manual manipulation to get the exact views needed for leadership, finance, or audit purposes" (Verified User, Jun 2026); graphics ordering/grouping complaints (Jul 2025).
- **Benchmark gaps for variable-pay roles:** "benchmark mapping feature currently shows base salary and doesn't yet incorporate OTE" for sales roles (Michelle H., Dec 2025).
- **Audit-log gap on edit chains (older):** wished-for "ability to track changes made to salary increase recommendations by Reviewing Manager > One Up Manager > Approver (audit log on those fields)" (Aug 2023) — notable since "Audit log" is now a marketed analytics feature; field-level change tracking depth is [uncertain].
- **Budget red-flag false alarms** (Molly S., Mar 2026 — quoted above) and **manual data upload with no template** ("we found it confusing that the upload was flexible with no template provided" — Marwa H.).
- **Integration friction:** "Our only real pain point was on the integration side, which made some parts of the process feel clunky" (Olivia F., Mar 2026); PEO performance tools not supported (Nina A.).
- Vendor's own FAQ concedes: "Market data coverage may be limited for highly niche roles, and human judgment is often required to supplement benchmarks" (https://www.comprehensive.io/faq).

## Data, benchmarks & methodology (the scraped-ranges dataset)

- **Collection:** "We built software that automatically visits the Careers pages of these companies every day, finds all the job posts, and uses AI to extract the salary ranges from the text of the job descriptions… a system to efficiently use GPT-4 for this purpose, including auto-crafting prompts and performing pre and post-processing." Stack: React / Node.js / Postgres (Roger Lee, quoted in https://blog.pragmaticengineer.com/the-scoop-salary-transparency/).
- **Scale trajectory:** 700+ companies / 53k+ postings at Jan-2023 launch (HR Dive) → 2,000 companies by Apr 2023 (Pragmatic Engineer) → **6,000+ US tech companies, refreshed daily** today (https://www.comprehensive.io/products/benchmarking). US-only; sourced from legally mandated disclosures (CA, NYC, WA, CO…).
- **Normalization:** postings are mapped to **job families and standardized levels (IC1–IC5+ style with human labels, e.g., "IC3 (Proficient)")**, then aggregated to an average posted min–max per level, date-stamped monthly (observed meta on app.comprehensive.io/benchmarking/postings). Insight from the dataset: average posted range $130k–$200k = ±21% from midpoint, matching HR best practice for internal band width; ML Engineer postings average 19% above Software Engineer (Pragmatic Engineer interview).
- **Caveats:** posted ranges ≠ paid salaries; "good faith" width varies (Netflix $300k–$900k outliers); equity/bonus excluded from posting ranges. Levels are inferred by Comprehensive, not declared by employers. [uncertain — current inference method]
- **Other datasets:** internal dataset "informed by anonymized customer data and publicly available market information"; Mercer Comptryx (4M+ employees, 100+ countries, tech sub-industries); Salary.com (9M+ employees "sourced directly from HR departments and refreshed monthly" — homepage); exec survey (500+ VC-backed companies, individual anonymized points, validated by comp experts, year-round refresh). Sample cuts available pre-sale (FAQ). Free press validation: CNBC, Bloomberg, Forbes, NYT all cited the dataset (links on homepage).

## Patterns worth borrowing for Advisor Comp Studio

1. **Justification-gated overrides.** Any action exceeding a guardrail (cap, range, budget) triggers a configurable justification prompt that travels with the approval record. Map directly onto compa-ratio guardrail breaches in tier assignment: breach → inline reason field → reason rendered in the governance checklist and audit log.
2. **Soft warning vs. hard cap duality per field** — and the lesson from the G2 budget-red-flag complaint: scope warning colors to the *aggregate* constraint, not each line item, so a single over-guideline advisor grant doesn't scream red if the pool is fine.
3. **"Employees don't see changes until letters are released."** A clean draft/published state machine: all mid-cycle edits are invisible to the recipient surface until an explicit release. Mirrors exactly what the watermarked Proposition needs — internal scenario churn never leaks into the advisor-facing doc until "release."
4. **Web-first letter with optional PDF + acknowledgement tracking.** Award letters are web documents with PDF export, delivery/acknowledgement status, and bulk reminders. The Proposition doc could adopt the acknowledgement-tracking ledger (sent → viewed → acknowledged) feeding the audit log.
5. **Individual anonymized data points over averages** (exec survey). For advisor value-band anchors, show the scatter of comparable grants with percentile overlays rather than a single midpoint — far more persuasive in a board discussion.
6. **Level-ladder percentile view.** Benchmarking's "unified view of percentile data across all levels" is a one-screen ladder (level on Y, range bars on X). Direct fit for FAST tiers: one chart showing all four tiers' BPS ranges with the proposed grant's marker.
7. **As-of stamping on every figure.** Tracker pages bake "(as of Jun 2026)" into the headline figure. Stamp every Proposition figure and scenario output with engine version + as-of date — cheap defensibility.
8. **Range bars with position markers + compa-ratio dot** as the standard band visualization (their pay-ranges screen) — cleaner than tables for showing where an advisor's package sits inside a tier band.
